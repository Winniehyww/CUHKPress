# FEAR APPROACH IN ADVERTISING MAY NOT WORK, EXPERT SAYS
**Date:** 1993-05-13
**Content:**
Prof. Richard Bagozzi from the University of Michigan, speaking at the
Chinese University of Hong Kong’s 30th Anniversary Lecture, argued that
the "fear appeal" in advertising (e.g., highlighting tooth decay) often
fails. His research on anti-child-abuse ads showed high-fear messages
are less persuasive than moderate fear with empathy. He also discussed
ongoing studies on bone marrow donation motivations with CUHK’s Prof.
Lee Kam Hon.
